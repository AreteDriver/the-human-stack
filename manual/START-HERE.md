# Start Here

This is the guided path through The Human Stack.

## How to Read This Reference

The Human Stack is not a book to read cover-to-cover. It is a reference to return to repeatedly. Follow the path that matches your role and intent.

## Paths

### Path 1: "I am evaluating a senior engineer."

1. [70-engineering-reviews/](70-engineering-reviews/) — How they think through reversals and failures.
2. [10-case-studies/](10-case-studies/) — What they shipped and how they measured it.
3. [20-evaluation/](20-evaluation/) — How they know their systems work.

### Path 2: "I am building AI systems in production."

1. [30-operations/](30-operations/) — Runbooks, playbooks, and operational patterns.
2. [20-evaluation/](20-evaluation/) — How to evaluate models, agents, and deployments.
3. [40-architecture/](40-architecture/) — Architecture patterns and tradeoffs.

### Path 3: "I am designing an AI system from scratch."

1. [50-principles/](50-principles/) — The design philosophy.
2. [40-architecture/](40-architecture/) — Proven patterns.
3. [20-evaluation/](20-evaluation/) — How to validate before you build.

### Path 4: "I want to understand the methodology."

1. [00-introduction/](00-introduction/) — What The Human Stack is and why it exists.
2. [50-principles/](50-principles/) — The core ideas.
3. [70-engineering-reviews/](70-engineering-reviews/) — How those ideas evolved.

## Reading Philosophy

- **Start with evidence.** Every chapter carries an evidence block at the top. Read it first.
- **Check the maturity.** See [MATURITY.md](../MATURITY.md) for the maturity of capabilities.
- **Follow references.** Chapters link to related chapters. The manual is a graph, not a list.
- **Question everything.** If evidence is weak, treat the claim as a hypothesis.
