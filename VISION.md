# Vision

## Mission

Build the most evidence-based public reference on engineering operational AI systems.

## The Problem

Most AI engineering knowledge exists in three places:

1. **Private company playbooks** — inaccessible, tribal, lost when people leave.
2. **Academic papers** — rigorous but disconnected from production constraints.
3. **Blog posts and tweets** — timely, but unversioned, unbenchmarked, and often wrong six months later.

There is no public, living reference that treats AI deployment as an engineering discipline with the same rigor as distributed systems, reliability engineering, or manufacturing operations.

## The Approach

We treat AI systems as **operational systems**:

- Designed, not assembled.
- Measured, not assumed.
- Governed, not improvised.
- Improved continuously, not shipped and forgotten.

This perspective connects:
- Manufacturing operations (Lean, TPS)
- Software reliability (SRE, observability)
- AI infrastructure (agents, evaluation, governance)

into a single engineering discipline.

## What Success Looks Like

In five years, an engineer deploying an agentic system should be able to say:

> "We followed The Human Stack's deployment lifecycle, used its evidence framework to grade our claims, and referenced its case studies to avoid known failure modes."

And that engineer should not be us.

## Non-Goals

- **Prompt collections.** Prompts are implementation details, not engineering principles.
- **AI hype.** We do not publish speculative predictions about AGI timelines or capabilities.
- **Vendor marketing.** We evaluate tools and platforms on evidence, not press releases.
- **News commentary.** We do not react to every model release or funding round.

## Principles

1. **Evidence over enthusiasm.** Every claim carries an evidence grade. Claims without evidence are labeled as hypotheses.
2. **Uncertainty is a feature.** We explicitly state confidence levels and known unknowns. That increases trust, not decreases it.
3. **Version the thinking.** Ideas evolve. We make that evolution visible, not hidden.
4. **Failures teach more than successes.** Engineering Reviews include abandoned designs, unexpected outcomes, and reversed decisions.
5. **Reference implementations are evidence, not the product.** Animus, drift-monitor, and memboot exist to prove that the principles in this manual work in practice.

## Governance

This project is maintained as an open engineering reference. Corrections, additions, and evidence upgrades are welcome via pull request. Editorial standards are defined in [CONSTITUTION.md](CONSTITUTION.md).
